# Manor — Design Context

*For Impeccable and its action skills. Loaded automatically by `impeccable craft`, `shape`, `critique`, etc.*

---

## Users

**Manor is one person's tool.** Hana — writer, novelist, NYT-featured. Uses it on her macOS desktop, privately, every day. Nobody else logs in. No accounts, no onboarding, no marketing surfaces.

**Context of use:**
- Morning review at a desk over coffee, evening glance before bed.
- While writing (so: must never interrupt flow, never beg for attention).
- Occasionally mid-task on the same machine as Scrivener, Pages, Ulysses — Manor lives next to those, not as a system-tray poke.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Hold the day in one place (tasks, calendar, rhythm, chores, ledger).
- Reflect on the month (MonthReviewPanel, AI narrative).
- Track household state without turning into a spreadsheet.
- Keep a gentle relationship with time and money — not a productivity ratchet.

---

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** *cottagecore*, *handmade*, *quiet*.

Manor is a well-kept household journal, not a dashboard. It's the feeling of leather-bound planners, ledger paper, pressed-flower marginalia, a woodgrain kitchen drawer. Warm, tactile, close. Slightly old-fashioned in a deliberate way — the way a good novel chooses its period.

**Emotional goal:** calm, private, cared-for. Opening Manor should feel like opening a favourite notebook — not like being pinged.

**Voice in the UI:** understated, human, a little literary. "~ the morning ~" not "TODO: AM TASKS". "£760 remaining this month" not "Budget: 38% utilised". Nothing clinical, nothing chirpy.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Cottage journal.** Cream paper base, ink text, ivy + rust accents, ornamental typography for section partitions, hand-lettered accents for dates and pullquotes. Monospace for times and numbers. Dark pullquote panels for emphasis only — the SummaryCard stays as a deliberate exception, not a default.

### Palette (OKLCH; replaces iMessage tokens for new components)

| Token | OKLCH | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| `--paper` | `oklch(0.97 0.008 70)` | Warm cream base — ivory, slight yellow tint |
| `--oat` | `oklch(0.93 0.012 75)` | Slightly deeper surface for stacked cards |
| `--ink` | `oklch(0.22 0.02 60)` | Warm black — never `#000` |
| `--ink-soft` | `oklch(0.45 0.02 60)` | Secondary text, warm gray |
| `--hairline` | `oklch(0.85 0.01 70 / 0.4)` | Barely-there borders |
| `--ivy` | `oklch(0.42 0.09 150)` | Primary accent — muted deep green |
| `--rust` | `oklch(0.52 0.13 35)` | Emphasis / alert — warm terracotta |
| `--butter` | `oklch(0.75 0.10 90)` | Success / ripe — warm yellow |
| `--plum` | `oklch(0.35 0.08 340)` | Rare special moments only |

All neutrals tinted toward brand-warm (hue ~70). No cool grays. No pure black. No pure white.

**Dark surfaces** (SummaryCard-style pullquote panels): `oklch(0.18 0.02 60)` to `oklch(0.22 0.03 55)` gradient, ink-cream body inverted to cream-on-ink. Used sparingly — 1-2 per view, for emphasis only.

### Typography

- **Display serif** (section headings, greetings, counts): **Marcellus** (Google) or **PP Right Serif Narrow** (paid). Warm uppercase, wide tracking. *Not* Fraunces, Playfair, DM Serif — see anti-patterns.
- **Body**: macOS system stack (`-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont`). SF Pro. Keep it native, keep it fast.
- **Handwritten accent** (dates, ornamental markers, pullquotes): **Caveat** (Google) — warm, flowing. For dates like "*the seventh of April*", signatures, day-partition labels.
- **Monospace accent** (times, currency, line numbers, counts): **Commit Mono** or **JetBrains Mono**. Used for numbers *only* — never for body.

Type scale (rem, fixed — not fluid, per Impeccable guidance for app UIs):

```
--text-xs:  0.75rem   12px  (captions, metadata)
--text-sm:  0.875rem  14px  (secondary body)
--text-md:  1rem      16px  (body)
--text-lg:  1.25rem   20px  (subheadings)
--text-xl:  1.625rem  26px  (section headings)
--text-2xl: 2.25rem   36px  (greetings, day partitions)
--text-3xl: 3rem      48px  (hero moments — rare)
```

At least 1.25 ratio between consecutive steps. Display-serif sizes use a touch more tracking (`letter-spacing: 0.01em` to `0.03em`).

### Spatial

- Existing 4pt scale stays: `--space-sm/md/lg` etc.
- Generous margins — this is a journal, not a dashboard. Err toward whitespace.
- Column width capped ~70ch for body prose.
- **Variation matters**: morning/afternoon/evening partitions should *visually breathe*. Not identical cards stacked.
- Asymmetric over symmetric. Left-aligned over centered. Break the grid for emphasis (a date in the margin, a pullquote that extends past the column).

### Ornamental Language

Manor earns small, consistent typographic ornaments:

- `✦` — day / section marker (start of Today, start of a ledger month)
- `❦` — footer / closing flourish
- `~ the morning ~` / `~ after tea ~` / `~ the evening ~` — day partitions (italic, soft ink)
- `—` em-dash for soft separators
- No emoji in chrome. Emoji stays inside user content (notes, reactions) where it belongs.

### Motion

- Minimal. Transitions should feel like pages turning, not bounces.
- `ease-out-quart` or `ease-out-expo` only. No bounce/elastic — see anti-patterns.
- Reduced-motion respected everywhere. On `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`, fades stay, slides collapse to instant.
- No scroll-driven shaders. No parallax. No staggered hero reveals. Manor doesn't show off.

## Design Principles

1. **Journal, not dashboard.** If a new surface starts looking like Stripe / Linear / Notion, stop. Ask what a *page in a beautiful planner* would look like instead.
2. **Whitespace is content.** Cramped layouts feel corporate. Give Manor room to breathe even at the cost of screen real estate.
3. **Tint every neutral.** Pure grays, pure blacks, pure whites are banned. Everything has a whisper of brand warmth.
4. **Numbers get a typographic voice.** Times, currency, counts use monospace. It's how Manor shows respect for data without dashboard-flavored chart decoration.
5. **One accent at a time.** Ivy *or* rust in a surface, not both. Colour is rare by design — that's what gives it weight.
6. **Hand-lettered accents are seasoning, not structure.** Caveat stays in dates, day partitions, pullquotes. Body stays system-stack.
7. **Dark panels are for emphasis, not default.** The SummaryCard-style dark gradient is a highlight device, not a theme. Don't retrofit dark panels as "cool-looking cards".
8. **Anti-corporate, anti-chirpy.** No "Great job!" toasts, no confetti, no badges. Manor doesn't high-five.

## Anti-References

What Manor must *never* look like:

- **SaaS dashboards** — gradient KPI tiles, identical cards in a grid, charts everywhere, "week over week ↗ 12%".
- **AI tech-bro** — dark-mode-with-cyan-glow, purple-to-blue gradients, "AI-powered" badges, sparkle icons, Inter everywhere.
- **Corpo productivity** — Microsoft 365 chrome, Google Calendar blandness, enterprise-safe grays.
- **Consumer iOS cloneware** — rounded cards nested in rounded cards, pastel gradients, bounce animations, Apple Health card sprawl.

Technical anti-patterns Manor refuses (enforced by `audit`):

- Gradient text (`background-clip: text`).
- Coloured side-stripe borders (`border-left: 3px solid ...`).
- Glassmorphism as decoration.
- `border-radius: 9999px` on everything.
- Icons with rounded-square backgrounds above every heading.
- Inter, Roboto, Playfair, Fraunces, DM Sans, IBM Plex, Space Grotesk, Outfit. (Reflex fonts — banned.)
- Pure `#000` / `#fff`. Pure grays without hue tint.
- Bounce / elastic easing.
- Modals when a side panel or inline reveal would do.

## Accessibility

- macOS desktop, single user, so pragmatic: no keyboard-only requirement beyond basic tab order, no screen-reader certification target.
- **Reduced motion**: respected everywhere. Non-negotiable.
- **Contrast**: WCAG AA (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large). The cream-on-ink palette gives generous margin here. Verify in `audit` reports.
- **Text sizing**: respect macOS Display → Text Size. Don't fight it with viewport-locked `px`.
- **Colour-blindness**: don't rely on colour alone. Ivy/rust distinctions always paired with a typographic or shape cue.

## Current State (as of 2026-04-17)

The app currently mixes two systems:

- **Legacy iMessage tokens** (`--imessage-blue`, `--imessage-green`) in older components from Phase 2-3.
- **Paper/ink base tokens** (`--paper`, `--ink`, `--hairline`) that align with this Design Context.
- **Dark gradient surfaces** (`#1a1a2e → #16213e`) introduced in Phase 5 for the SummaryCard. These should migrate to the warm dark palette `oklch(0.18 0.02 60)` above; the iMessage blues should migrate to ivy/rust.

Migration isn't retrofit-all-at-once. New work commits to the Cottage Journal direction. Existing components get refactored when they're touched for other reasons (opportunistic), or via an `/impeccable extract` pass later.
